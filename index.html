About Me

I received both my bachelors and masters degrees in electrical engineering from the University of Southern California. During my time there, I was fortunate enough to be able to work with many different researchers on a variety of projects ranging from vehicle to vehicle communication, neuroimaging, and wildlife classification.

My professional/academic interests lie in researching and applying computer vision to problems in the real world both in engineering and the sciences. Currently, this has taken the form of working on applied research for self-driving cars. However, I would ideally love to work on problems related to conservation and ecology. My personal interests include hiking, backpacking, coffee, history, and literature. I've only been on a handful of backpacking trips, but I can say that it is among one of the most liberating ways to spend my free time... unless you forget bug spray.

As of now, I am working at Honda Research Institute where I am working on perception and prediction research topics related to the overall problem of self-driving cars. Perception in this case meaning how the car can perceive and make sense of the world around it, and prediction meaning how can we reason about the other agent's intentions in this dynamic environment.

I'm not sure what the future holds, but I am sure that I very much so enjoy research and the exploration of open ended problems generally within the realm of applied machine learning and computer vision. This could end up with me remaining put and doing research at various research labs in industry. Or, more than likely, I will end up applying for (and hopefully being accepted by) a PhD program after my time at HRI comes to a close.
