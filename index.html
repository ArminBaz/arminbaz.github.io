About Me

I received both my bachelors and masters degrees in electrical engineering from the University of Southern California. During my time there, I was fortunate enough to be able to work with many different researchers on a variety of projects ranging from vehicle to vehicle communication, neuroimaging, and wildlife classification.

My interests include hiking, backpacking, artificial intelligence (generally), computer vision, video games, history, literature, and coffee. Of everything I listed I spend the most time on video games, especially now that I am working full time. Perhaps I will remedy this in the future, but I am rather engrossed in the world of Elden Ring at the moment.

Currently, I am working at Honda Research Institute where I am working on perception and prediction research topics related to the overall problem of self-driving cars. Perception in this case meaning how the car can perceive and make sense of the world around it, and prediction meaning how can we reason about the other agent's intentions in this dynamic environment.

I'm not sure what the future holds, but I am sure that I very much so enjoy research and the exploration of open ended problems generally within the realm of applied machine learning and computer vision. This could end up with me remaining put and doing research at various research labs in industry. Or, more than likely, I will end up applying for a PhD after my time at HRI comes to a close.
