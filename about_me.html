About Me

I received both my bachelors and masters degrees in electrical engineering from the University of Southern California. During my time there, I was fortunate enough to be able to work with many different researchers on a variety of projects.

I am very passionate about the natural world. I love backpacking and hiking and I believe these are among the greatest ways to enjoy nature. Although there is no "correct" way to enjoy nature I find myself most ~connected~ doing these. Connected in this sense less in the kumbaya spiritual way and more in the meditative way of feeling at peace. One of my greater passions is also making sandwiches. It might sound simple at first, but making a good sandwich is an art in itself. I also enjoy learning about ancient history.

Academically, I have developed more than a passing interest in the intersection of the fields of neuroscience, artificial intelligence, and cognitive science. On the more engineering side of things I find brain-machine-interfaces to be absolutely fascinating and I think the potential use cases of these things is amazing. On the more theoretical, yet still quite grounded in some ways side, I am deeply interested in how we can use clues about how human intelligence works to help advance the state of the art in artificial intelligence and vice versa.
