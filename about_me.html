About Me

I received both my bachelors and masters degrees in electrical engineering from the University of Southern California. During my time there, I was fortunate enough to be able to work with many different researchers on a variety of projects.

I am very passionate about the natural world, I love backpacking and hiking and I believe these are among the greatest ways to enjoy nature. Although there is no "correct" way to enjoy nature I find myself most ~connected~ doing these. One of my greater passions is also making sandwiches, it might sound simple at first, but making a good sandwich is an art in itself. I also enjoy learning about ancient history, although I absolutely despise people who are only interested in imperialist history, it is a big part of ancient history so maybe this is just a bit of my self-loathing coming out.

Academically, I have developed more than a passing interest in the intersection of the fields of neuroscience, artificial intelligence, and cognitive science. Specifically, my interests lie within vision, how do humans make sense of the natural word through our eyes? How do we quickly learn to recognize objects and understand complex 3D shapes? And, most importantly, how do we bestow this unto machines? Therefore, I would say my primary interest is in the field of computer vision, but I believe there is a lot of insight to be drawn from biology, specifically the human visual system. An ideal area of research, for me, is one where I can use inspiration from neuroscience to address fundamental research problems in the field of computer vision. Things like the long-tailed distribution problem, incomplete or imperfect data quality, and fine grained visual categorization to name a few.
