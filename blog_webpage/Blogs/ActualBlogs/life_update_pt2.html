Life Update pt. 2

A little over two years since my last update, holy shit. I know everyone reading this has been waiting for another steaming hot life so here it is.

I honestly don't even know where to begin, there's been so much that happened. I got a job at Honda Research Institute, I had my first publication in a conference that I never thought I'd even be able to publish in, and I am now pursuing my PhD in a field that I truly never thought I would be able to partake in. Don't worry, this isn't going to be some sort of motivational "You can do whatever you set your mind to!" type thing, although I do believe that to a certain extent. I'm jusy trying to detail how much has happened in the last two years.

Let's start with HRI. It was a dream job. That's all there really is to say about it. I was in the right place at the right time and I got the opportunity to do cutting edge self-driving car research at a company with plenty of "fuck you" money to throw at the problem. My work life balance was unparalleled, and to be honest it will most likely never be like that again. I had amazing and supportive colleagues who actually cared about my well-being. And most importantly, I learned so much, not just about research, but about myself and what I really wanted out of life.

To tie into that epic last sentence, I'll talk about my PhD. The field of computational and theoretical neuroscience has long been a huge interest for me. When I was still doing my undergraduate degree at USC I would frequently read papers and watch talks about the field, I even let that interest carry me into reinforcement learning for a bit as the ties between RL and neuroscience (see DeepMind) were particularly fascinating. Unfortunately, I put these interests aside because I was already so deep into my engineering degree and my computer vision research that I didn't think a shift like this was possible.

Now, I'm happy to say that I am pursuing a PhD in Psychology again at USC where I will be conducting research in computational and theoretical neuroscience. To say it wasn't fortuitious is an understatement, I got super lucky. I knew I wanted to do a PhD, only this time around I convinced myself that it would be worth the shot to apply to neuroscience/psych/cogsci programs as well. During one of my manic professor exploration episodes I managed to stumble upon my current PhD advisor, Payam Piray. I somehow managed to convince him, and USC, that I would a good doctoral student and now here we are.

This was honestly the best case scenario for me. I was only applying to schools in Southern California because I didn't want to move my girlfriend, nor myself, half way across the country and away from our families. On top of that I get to stay in Los Angeles, a city that I have deeply fallen in love with. The icing on the cake is that I will be doing the exact form of research that has long interested me, using inspiration and ideas from Bayesian Decision Theory and reinforcement learning to develop computational models of the brain.
