Corona Virus Documentation

The whole purpose of this blog post is so I can have some written documentation of what I am currently feeling during the covid19 corona virus pandemic. It occured to me that I have not actually written my thoughts or feelings about this and I think it would be interesting to be able to read this after everything is over to be able to put myself back into the shoes of Spring 2020 Armin.

We were sitting down for a mother's day brunch (courtesy of my sister, almost everything was vegan and tasted phenomenal), when the conversation shifted to writing in journals and why we do it. I used to be very good at writing in my journal, when I first went to USC I would write in there almost every other day sometimes I would write everyday for a few weeks. Journaling is a great way to get things off your chest and help sort through your ideas when the times are rough. Nowadays I'm so happy with my studies, my friends and family, and most importantly my girlfriend, that the thought of journaling hasn't crossed my mind in a long while. Strange, but not really. Marcus brought up a good point however. He said, "Why limit yourself to only writing things that aren't so good, you should also want to write down when you are feeling happy about things too." To his credit, that is a fantastic idea. Journaling shouldn't be about just writing about things that upset you. You should write about anything and everything so that when you look back on them, you have a timeline of your life.

Because this time of pandemic is so novel, I thought today I would write about my personal experience with the coronavirus.

To be completely honest. I think this virus has changed my life for the better. Not because I got it and turned into a super mutant (although that would be sick), but because it really showed me what's important in life. Cliche, I know, but it's true. Shifting everything online and being at home really gives you time to step back from your previous daily routine and examine your past life through a different lense. I see now how much I value spending time with my friends and family. I see now how much I truly enjoy being outdoors and being active and how important that is for my mental health, I see now how amazing learning can be when you don't have the pressure of studying for a grade and you are free to build mental maps of whatever interests you. This pandemic taught me many things, but I think above all it has taught me how to be a better friend, brother, son, boyfriend, and overall human.

Now, because I don't want to leave it off on such a cheesy note, I'll talk a little bit about the direct impacts of the virus. It's fucking crazy. Especially when it first started spreading. Everything started shutting down, the streets became barren, and the grocery stores turned into warzones. You don't really get to appreciate the sanity of most people until you see the insanity of a few. People were freaking out, for good reason I'll admit, but at the same time it's not really conducive behavior for anything other than creating more panic.

I still remember being on spring break and watching the emails come in with decreasing intervals informing the students about the current state of affaris. Each one read the same "Something something unprecedented times, something something extending online classes for x ammount of time." Eventually it got to the point where they told us classes would be online at least until the upcoming Fall semester. I have a feeling fall semester is going to be online too I don't see any reason to rush back. The risk definitely doesn't outweigh the reward in this situation.

Weird weird times. But also great times nonetheless? I'll keep updating this as time goes on.
