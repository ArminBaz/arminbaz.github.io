Paper Read Through: Imitating Latent Policies from Observation

Link to the paper.

Context:

An accepted paper from Professer Daniel Yammins at Stanford to NIPS 2020. The problem they are solving is achieving state of the art results with a network that is more inspired, and similar to, the brain.


Motivation:

The authors main objective was to create a convolutional artificial neural network (ANN) that was more anatomically correct with the alignment of the brain. They want to show that high performance on machine learning tasks can be more inline with neuroscience too. They develop CORnet-S a shallow ANN with four anatomically mapped areas {V1, V2, V4, IT} and recurrent connectivity guided by Brain-Score. Brain-Score is a behavorial benchmark that scores any ANN on how similar it is to the brain's mechanisms for core object recognition.
One might ask, "why is this useful?" This is useful because having a strong model of the brain opens up a door to a new world of non-invasive brain centered technology. For example, brain-machine interfaces. The authors of the paper also make clear that pure performance on ImageNet does not correlate to brain likeliness. On top of that, the link between hundreds of layers (ResNet) or complex branching structures (Inception and NasNet) and the areas of the brain's ventral stream are not obvious.
