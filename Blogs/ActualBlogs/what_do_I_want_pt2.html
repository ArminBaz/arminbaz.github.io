What do I want, pt. 2

Welcome to the second installment of what do I want to do with my life. So, for the past days I have been running around in circles with one single question. "Do I want to pursue a PHD?" I think in my heart, I already know the answer. But, there are many things to consider, and I am hoping that by taking the time to write a blog post about it, I will be able to clearly lay out my thoughts and all the pros and cons of this decision.

Essentially, the main reason I tell myself that I want to pursue a PhD is because if I want to do research, then I might as well just get a PhD. Now, I will address this line of reasoning because I believe there are a lot of things wrong it.

So, I don't even know if I want to do research. How can I know I want to do something when I haven't worked as an engineer nor as a researcher? The main reason I think that I want to do research is mostly because it sounds sexy, because that's what is scaring me. It sounds cool and sexy.

After giving this some more thought. I have actually came to the realization that I really don't want to pursue a PhD. At least, not right now. When I asked myself "What would you be doing right now if you could do anything, what could you be working on that would make you the most happy?" I realized that I would be most happy if I actually create something. If I can actually build something that will help benefit humanity in some way. That is what I think I would find most fulfilling. The example I like to give is that I would rather be the person who creates the self driving car, rather than the person who helped come up with the idea behind the CNN, the neural network architecture that the self driving car is using.

I think there is a reason that I wanted to get into engineering in the first place and I shouldn't be too quick to throw all of that behind me just because of some stupid notion that you have to have a PhD to be smart or to actually be able to work on something meaningful. First of all, that is completely false. And second of all, if that was my main motivation for getting a doctorate degree, I would be getting into it for the wrong reasons.

Okay cool, so you don't want a PhD, now what?

Honestly this is one of the hardest questions for me to answer. I really don't know. All I do know is that I want to work on something really cool. Maybe that means self-driving cars, maybe that means working on robots, I still don't exactly know. What I do know is that for the first time in a while, I actually feel confident in the direction that I want my life to go into. On top of that, it's super nice to not have worry about grades as much as I would have to if I want pursue a PhD. Instead I can really focus on making sure that I learn a lot from my classes. I know that is already supposed to be a given, but when you are worrying about grades you really can't enjoy a class as much as someone who simply wanted to be in there to learn. That's just a fact of life. If you have some added pressure on something, you probably won't enjoy it as much, which means you also won't get as much out of it as you could be. It's a pretty sad and nefarious cycle, and one that I am glad to finally be breaking free from.
