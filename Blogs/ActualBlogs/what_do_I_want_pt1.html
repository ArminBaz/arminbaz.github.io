What do I want, pt. 1

This blog was originally supposed to be a place where I can get my feelings out in a somewhat "public" place. I'm not even sure why I feel the need to have this be out there. Of course I could write this in a notebook or type it on a hidden document on my laptop, but there's a different feeling that comes with the knowledge that perhaps someone may stumble upon this. In a way, I feel like the underground man. The knowledge of the underlying what if motivates me in a somewhat perverse way to more fully flesh out my thoughts and be sure that I completely agree with what I am putting down. In many ways, I think this is a good thing. If I am to ponder about what I want to do with my life and why I want to do certain things, it is best to flesh it out to the finest detail is it not?

Now that I gave such a dramatic introduction anything else would feel wrong. I'll begin about what I want to study for my PhD (if I'm fortunate enough to get accepted anywhere) and why I even feel the need to get PhD in the first place.

I was thinking, I have been thinking, quite a lot about what I want to study if I were to get a PhD. I know what I like. I know that I really enjoy machine learning, specifically deep learning. I like electrical engineering, but mostly when it is in line with machine learning (signal processing), some embedded systems and robotics. Neuroscience, but not when it gets too into the nitty gritty of the chemistry and behind it. And psychology.

Now what do you get when you combine all of the above? Nothing really. But there seems to be a growing body of research on combining engineering, neuroscience, and deep learning as a way to study the mind and also as a way to further each of the respective afformentioned fields. This would include anyone at the Center for Brains Minds and Machines (CBMM), professor Dan Yammins from Stanford, Niftali Tishby in Israel, Christof Koch at the Allen Institute, among many others.
